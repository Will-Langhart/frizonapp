overview of each platform and the steps to build a website using Shopify, Wix, and GoDaddy.

Shopify: Shopify is an ecommerce platform that allows you to build and customize your online store easily. Here are the steps to build a website using Shopify:

  1. Sign up for a Shopify account: Go to the Shopify website and sign up for an account. You'll need to choose a plan that suits your business needs and budget.

  2. Choose a theme: Shopify has a variety of themes to choose from. Pick one that fits your brand and customize it according to your liking.

  3. Add products: Add your products to your Shopify store. You can add product images, descriptions, prices, and more.

  4. Set up payment options: Set up payment options for your customers. Shopify supports a variety of payment methods, including credit cards, PayPal, and Apple Pay.

  5. Set up shipping: Set up shipping rates for your products. You can choose from a variety of shipping methods, including free shipping, flat rate shipping, and carrier calculated shipping.

  6. Launch your website: Once you've completed all the above steps, your website is ready to launch. You can start promoting your products and driving traffic to your online store.

Wix: Wix is a website builder that allows you to create a website without coding knowledge. Here are the steps to build a website using Wix:

  2. Choose a template: Wix has a variety of templates to choose from. Pick one that fits your brand and customize it according to your liking.

  3. Add content: Add content to your website, including text, images, videos, and more. Wix has a drag-and-drop editor that makes it easy to add and edit content.

  4. Add features: Wix has a variety of features to add to your website, including forms, social media icons, and more. Add the features that are most important for your business.

  5. Set up SEO: Wix allows you to optimize your website for search engines. Add keywords, meta descriptions, and other SEO elements to improve your website's visibility.

  6. Launch your website: Once you've completed all the above steps, your website is ready to launch. You can start promoting your website and driving traffic to it.

    2. Install and set up the Transporter tool on your machine.

    Phased release for automatic updates is a feature in App Store Connect that allows you to gradually release an app update over a 7-day period to users who have turned on automatic updates for your app. This helps you to control the rate at which the update is pushed out to your users and can help you catch any potential issues before the update is rolled out to all users.

    It's important to note that the updated version of your app will still be available to all users as a manual update from the App Store during the phased release period. Additionally, you can pause the phased release for up to 30 days or release the update to all users at any time.

    To use the phased release feature in App Store Connect, you can follow these steps:

    1. Go to your app in App Store Connect.
    2. Select the "Activity" tab and find the version of your app that you want to release.
    3. Click the "Release This Version" button.
    4. Choose "Automatically release this version" and select the "Phased Release" option.
    5. Set the percentage of users that you want to receive the update each day, starting from 1% up to 100%.
    6. Click "Start Phased Release".

    Once you've started the phased release, you can monitor its progress in App Store Connect and make any necessary adjustments.

function activate(context) {
  let disposable = vscode.commands.registerCommand('extension.showGitStatus', async () => {
    const gitExtension = vscode.extensions.getExtension('vscode.git').exports;
    const git = gitExtension.getAPI(1);

    const repo = git.repositories[0];
    if (!repo) {
      vscode.window.showErrorMessage('No repository found.');
      return;
    }

    const status = await repo.getStatus();
    vscode.window.showInformationMessage(`Repository has ${status.length} changes.`);
  });

  context.subscriptions.push(disposable);
}
